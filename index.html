📖 Glossary of Terms

Cross-Correlation (r) Pearson correlation between HR and (inverted) SmO₂ during the active session. High values (>0.80) indicate strong structural cardiometabolic synchronization.
Phase Shift Time delay (lag) between the peak Heart Rate demand and the peak SmO₂ extraction nadir.
Wavelength / Period Average duration (seconds) to complete one full cycle (Sprint start to next Sprint start).
SmO₂ Interval Deterioration Difference in absolute SmO₂ drop between Sprint 1 and the Final Sprint. Positive = blunted extraction (fatigue).
HR Recovery Deterioration Difference in absolute HR drop between Recovery 1 and Final Recovery. Positive = parasympathetic withdrawal.

➗ Calculus & Detrended Sine Methodology

Model Independence: To prevent statistical bias caused by the discrepancy in local fitting models, the deterioration metrics are calculated using absolute raw amplitudes.

  • SmO₂ Deterioration Formula: |ΔSmO₂ Sprint 1 (Span)| - |ΔSmO₂ Sprint N (Span)|
  • HR Rec Deterioration Formula: |ΔHR Recovery 1 (Span)| - |ΔHR Recovery N (Span)|

Detrended Sine Wave Fitting: Physiological data during exercise naturally "drifts" (e.g., cardiac drift upward, or oxygenation baseline shift downward). If not corrected, this inclination forces the model to artificially guess "dampened" or "progressive" just to chase the drifting mean.

  • V8 Upgrade: The engine now calculates the linear baseline drift (inclination) of the entire session and subtracts it (Detrending). It then uses a grid-search to fit the pure oscillatory sine wave around this trendline. This guarantees the wave model accurately captures true amplitude behavior rather than mathematical drift.
  • Standard Sine: Consistent amplitude throughout all intervals.
  • Dampened Sine: The signal amplitude decays over time (e.g., progressive fatigue leading to blunted SmO₂ extraction or failure to fully recover HR).
  • Progressive Sine: The signal amplitude progressively increases over time (e.g., escalating physiological strain or warm-up effect).
